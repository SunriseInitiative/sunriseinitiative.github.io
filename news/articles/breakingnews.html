Nintendo's Updated Switch 2 EULA Grants Power to Disable Consoles Over Unauthorized Use

May 17, 2025 â€” Nintendo has revised its End User License Agreement (EULA) ahead of the Switch 2 launch, introducing significant changes that empower the company to disable consoles engaged in unauthorized activities. This move underscores Nintendo's intensified efforts to combat piracy, emulation, and hardware modifications.

In the updated agreement, Nintendo specifies that users are prohibited from actions such as reverse engineering, modifying, or circumventing any functions or protections of the Nintendo Account Services. Notably, the EULA states that violations may lead to the Nintendo Account Services and/or the applicable Nintendo device becoming permanently unusable, either wholly or partially. This clause effectively grants Nintendo the authority to "brick" consoles found in breach of the terms. Source: The Verge

Regional differences exist in the enforcement of these terms. While the U.S. version of the EULA includes the possibility of rendering devices unusable, the European version is more restrained due to consumer protection laws. In Europe, Nintendo's response to unauthorized use is limited to restricting access to pirated content, without the authority to disable the entire console. Source: MeriStation (AS)

Additionally, the updated EULA introduces an arbitration clause that restricts users from initiating class action lawsuits against Nintendo. Disputes must now be resolved through individual arbitration, eliminating the option for jury trials or class actions. Users have a 30-day window to opt out of this clause by submitting a written notice. Source: Polygon

These changes reflect Nintendo's proactive approach to safeguarding its intellectual property and maintaining control over its hardware ecosystem as it prepares for the Switch 2's release on June 5, 2025.
